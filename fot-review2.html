Introduction

The following is a reprint of an e-mail I received on October 22, AD 2001, by J. Edgell. For those of you who haven't seen Fallout Tactics yet, and are wondering what exactly it's like to play it, read on.

Another Review of Fallout Tactics: Brotherhood of Steel

Well most of us have played the Legendary "Wasteland." Most of us waited for years for a sequel and Fallout was born. Fallout 2 soon followed: the RPG of the Year! Now its Fallout Tactics. As we all know Interplay knows the right stuff in making an RPG with a post nuclear setting. Only Interplay. Fallout Tactics (FOT) is set as a sequel prequel. Confusing isn't it? Well FOT in story is the sequel to Fallout and a prequel to Fallout 2. Instead of being in the great Californ-i-a, you're in Chicago, home of the Cubs. Well it used to be home. Microforte developed most of the game. They changed the game from just "Turn Based" to "Turn Based and Real Time Strategy". You can switch the two during the game also. Most of how the game is played is just like Fallout... most of it anyway. You control a unit of men as the Brotherhood of Steel pretty much just shooting down whatever the Sargent tells ya to shoot. 20 missions is all you have. A lot of the Fallout feel is taken away with lack of deep interaction and being able to go were you want to go. The multiplayer function of FOT is the best of it all. Up to 17 players can play in one map at the same time! Choose a human, mutant, ghoul, dogs, and even a Deathclaw. You can also drive cars in the game such as a Hummer, tank, and many more. So my overall review of this game is that if it is that classic Fallout you're looking for, then FOT took a rocket launcher and blew it away. It is great for Online Gaming and I would recommend you playing to beat it if you are a Fallout completionist like I am. I would give it a 6 out of 10. Just for a note: It is said that Fallout 3 will be out. Lets just pray that its developed only by Interplay.

J. Edgell
