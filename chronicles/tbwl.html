Chapter T-Minus One and Counting
Past is Prologue: Department of Defense, U.S.A, Spring 1991

"Hey, Grimsly, you know why Iraqi tanks have rear view mirrors?"

A rather annoyed Kelsey Grimsly looked up from his computer terminal in the Department of Defense. He was currently trying to make a cross analysis of General Norman Schwarzkopf's Desert Storm strategies in comparison with the battle strategies the general had executed in other combat arenas. "I really do not know, Mr. Dymes, and I really do not care."

"So they can watch the battle! Get it? They're retreating and stuff? Oh forget it." Bob Dymes resigned himself to the fact that the only one in his office with a sense of humor was himself. Unless you count the paper shredder which had just shredded some vital documents of Kelsey's--by accident, of course.

Bob turned back to his computer screen, which was a map of Kuwait and all the burning oil wells Hussein had ignited. He was supposed to determine the most efficient and expedient route for clean up. He pressed a series of buttons, and to his delight, Super Tetris came back to the screen. "I love what you can do with computers these days," Bob thought to himself, as he began work on completing his 102nd line.

Suddenly, the computer screen flickered, then went blank. A red wave of guilt washed over his face. "Oh great, now what've I done!" he muttered. A nervous glance in his office-mate's direction indicated that his monitor had also blinked off. Bob thought he heard a small rumbling and glanced over at the file server housed against the wall.

Kelsey Grimsly narrowed his eyes at Bob, also known as Eclipse Bob, since he often crashed the entire network of SUN computers. Bob lumbered over to the ominously large file server to investigate the noise, opened the locked metallic sliding doors housing the IC boards, and peered in.

Inside, everything looked absolutely normal to Eclipse Bob. A microscopic scanning of the hardware and peripheral equipment would reveal otherwise, but Bob would have never guessed to order one of those. He shrugged his shoulders at Kelsey and was acknowledged by an exasperated Grimsly Grunt, and then he crawled back to his desk, vowing to erase Super Tetris off his hard drive--after he cleared a few more lines.

"Awww c'mon! Who erased my high score in Super Tetris??"

Soon Bob and Kelsey were absorbed in their work, but little did they realize that on the almost-microscopic level, a battle had been waged inside the guts of the SUN file server, and a virus had just been removed from the system, thanks to the anti-virus program. As the dust cleared, the anti-virus program merge-sorted through the rubble. All that was left of its virus prey was its potent weaponry code.

A binary bit stream sped to the massive SUN's CPU: "Virus 3D6A Neutralized. Query: Incorporate Weapon?"

"NEGATIVE. RETURN FOR REPAIR PATCHES."

Jax Yllandian and Taran Cyberpunk kept running through the strange maze of blinking lights, tall bare rectangular metallic trees, and gold pathways until Jax was out of breath, and they stopped behind a towering green wall crisscrossed with highlights in gold. Victory had eluded them this time, but they had in turn eluded those nasty tanks.

"You still functioning, Jax?" Taran inquired with a slight metallic accent.

"I'll live, T," Jax said between gasps, and piercing glances over his shoulder.

Taran snapped open a circuit patch on his left arm and started re-calibrating his motor neurons. "Good thing they like Ion Beamers, eh?" the half-Ranger, half- machine quipped between precisely regulated breaths.

It took Jax a minute to catch his breath after their taxing retreat. "Yeah. . . but now we're. . . weaponless in terra incognito. . . . I wonder if this place has a library. Let's go, T."

"Right behind you Jax."
