Chapter 1
Bernard Assaf

"Frak! Frak! Frak!"

The proprietor of the ammo shop in downtown Needles looked up from behind the check-out counter at the woman cursing at him. He tried to keep calm.

"What do you mean, you don't frakkin' carry clips for my Uzi anymore?" the woman continued, clearly irate.

"Well, it's just that we've, ah," the proprietor attempted to explain, "um, had some complaints about the thugs in the area, and your attempts at, eh, shall we say, 'enforcing the peace' with your somewhat, ah, violent methods are causing some of our townies to develop a bit of unrest. The town council has decided to limit the number of munitions that my establishment can have on-site, so that uh, desert warriors, such as yourself, are not as likely to buy them and use them for, ah, well, you understand. Do you, ah, you do understand, don't you?"

The proprietor took it as a bad sign that the woman unholstered the submachine gun from the leather strap at her side. He gulped audibly.

"I'm tempted to show you first hand my 'violent methods,'" the irate female quipped half under her breath.

"I tell you what," the proprietor began, boldly. "Why don't you leave your name and number, and I'll see what I can do about getting you some ammunition." His eyes glanced behind his customer to the armed guard he'd recently employed.

"I won't leave this place without ammo, Mister."

"Well, I can't sell you any ammo, so you won't be leaving this place with ammo, either." The clerk watched nervously as the guard stepped forward, flexing his muscles. The hired help had one hand on his holstered VP91Z.

"Excuse me," the guard spoke up. "But lady, if you don't treat my boss with some respect, you won't be leaving with or without your ammo."

The woman smiled at the clerk. She hadn't even turned around to face the guard. "Ok, you win. My name is Christina. Here's my number."

Quicker than the ejection of an empty round from a gun chamber, Christina swung around and stiff-armed the guard in the face, catching him completely by surprise, and breaking his nose. He dropped to his knees in pain, and tried to fumble for his weapon. Christina's next move was a swift upper kick to the man's side, as she ripped the VP91Z out of its holster and into the air. The gun sailed in the air in an arc over the guard's head toward the door, where Christina had run. She turned around, caught the gun, ripped out its clip, and threw the gun back to the guard.

"I'm tellin' ya, big boy. If I can't live with or without my ammo, I'll just take yours." She popped the 9mm clip into her Uzi, loaded it, flashed a grin at the clerk, who was near faint, and waved.

"Guess I'll have to take my business elsewhere. Have a nice day, fewmet-brains." And with that, the beautiful 'desert warrior' whirled around, her black hair whipping across her face, and left. There was someone she had to meet in the Acapulco Club.

Chapter 2
David Assaf

ACAPULCO. The door opened into Needles' seediest gaming establishment. Dim light diffusing weakly through the strata of stale smoky air made it difficult to assess the newcomer, but her natural saunter and purposeful gaze radiated a casual assurance that spoke volumes of the Uzi-sized bulge at her left breast, and her ability to utilize it.

Christina scanned the hall for her contact. Nothing obvious. "Playing it safe," she surmised, half smirking. Pushing her way through a copse of listless junkies, she made a bee-line to the gristly bartender. "How much for one squeezins, Spanky?"

"Twenty smackers, gorgeous."

"Frak it. Pour it my hand for a dime."

Their coded dialog concluded, Christina whipped her ponytail around and made for the corner of the hall Spanky had indicated. But the corner table was empty. Sitting down, she unfastened her shoulder holster and clapped it on the tabletop with an exasperated exhale. "It's a wonder they can keep their bootstraps tied, much less keep their own appointments." A sudden commotion at one of the poker tables caught her eye, just as a tired hooker with an ill-fitting wig plopped down against the nearby wall. "Bad hair day?" Christina offered, without averting her attention. A camo-bedecked mercenary had evidently left behind a sack of winnings, and was trotting toward another poker table. The dealer kept on calling after him in an irritating nasal voice, but the gambler ignored him and played a few more hands at the second table.

Christina finally looked over and down at the callgirl encircling her knees with her arms. She had to be having a hard go at it. The slobs around these parts paid for much fairer, fatter flesh. Through heavy mascara, she gazed at the gambling mercenary, no doubt attempting to estimate his (her?) potential earnings.

In disgust, Christina turned her attention back on her gambler. He was now back at the first dealer and was hastily digging through his recovered loot. Shrieking, he cursed at the first dealer, and then the second, and fumbled with his holster for a full second before Butkus the bouncer knocked his head into a meaty knee and dragged the loser outside. The hoard of junkies swiftly made away with the spilled loot in the commotion.

Christina was trying to make sense of it all when the prostitute spoke, "You know, he's doing it all wrong." That voice.... Contact.

"Frak, you make one ugly hooker! You think they could spare better makeup back at HQ."

"Nice to see you again, too. Frak, Chris, we just got our own bathroom last week. See what you're missing?"

"No thanks, Private. I ain't met ranger-meat yet that was worth his weight in fewmets, present company excepted of course."

Christina relaxed in her chair. "So what's up?"

Chapter 3
Luthen Iruun

"I'm trying to look not quite as uncomfortable as I really am."

"Good luck, Tammy. I mean, geez, you are playing a hooker."

"Yeah. I'm supposed to be watching out for this one guy imparticular." In the background of the casino the two women heard wood crashing and breaking and raised voices of men. "His name is Ishmael. He's a ranger, but he's gone missing after an unfortun-" Tammy was cut off as a table crashed into one of the walls of the establishment, having been thrown from across the room.

"LISTEN UP, RUNT! YOU OWE ME MONEY!!" From across the low-ceiled building a tall, massive desert scav was lifting up the nasal-voiced, bespeckled card dealer. The old man's gangly legs flailed off the floor.

"Please tell me that's not him."

"I hope not."

"Isn't he holding up..."

"Yeah, that's Rudolph. He's the third contact."

"Damnit to hell." Tammy agreed as she straightened her outfit and wig.

Christina unholstered her Uzi and unlocked the safety. "You armed?"

Tammy raised an eyebrow of disbelief. "You kidding me?!" Tammy rolled up her pant leg and pulled a 9mm automatic pistol from her bootcuff. She cocked it, then pulled a similar weapon from her other boot. "Let's dance."

Chapter 4
David Assaf

Rudolph's glasses lost their grip on his nose and landed with a clink on the broken poker table beneath him. The desert scav who'd just sunk $300 on five poorly played hands continued his stream of invectives, accentuated by fierce shakings which twice came close to dashing the dealer's head against the cracked, peeling ceiling. Rudolph began to wonder, as the burly scav began crushing the wind from his chest, what they'd write on his tombstone: Here lies Rudolph Blinn. Five-of-a-kind did him in.

His vision was blurred by myopia and motion, but from his vantage point, Rudolph soon noticed the crowd of gamblers and assorted scum parting behind his assailant. A new player had joined the game. She cleared her throat.

"Hey mister, he can cough it up better if you hold 'im upside down."

The scav swiveled his neck around owl-like and was greeted by the snub nose of an Uzi. The only sounds were the shuffling of junkies making a hasty exit to the street, and the crackling of Butkus' knuckles behind him, getting louder.

The Uzi rotated slightly around its barrel's axis, drawing his attention to the arm attached to it, and to the woman attached to the arm. The scav mumbled recognition. "I don't have a beef with you. Just want this rad rat turd to play fair."

Tammy giggled as she clicked her high-heeled boots across the room and twirled her left pistol. Her movement opened up Spanky's line of fire, and he chimed in from behind his sturdy counter as he noisily checked the clip in his M17, "Aww Rudy, you been bilkin' players again?"

The scav noted the bartender's lax attitude, as well as the profusion of weapons pointed in his direction. "People 'round here sure are cocky," he noted to himself. Kind of like you used to be, you SOB, his brain bubbled in response. "Yeah, but the sewers took care of that," he agreed silently.

Rudolph wiggled annoyingly, "Let ... me ... down ... you brute!" Cringing at his captive's grating nasal squeaks, the scav complied.

Compliance felt like a hard dusty splintered card table. In fact, it was a hard dusty splintered card table. Rudolph gasped for breath, a little over-dramatically, and began fumbling for his glasses among the poker chips and cards.

The scav turned toward Christina, his hands still in the air. "Guess I'll just be going now. This club reeks of fewmets."

Christina, surprised at the scav's self-control, motioned him onward with a silent flick of her weapon, after catching Spanky's shrug. Butkus stood his ground to force the scav to walk around him. As he brushed past, the bouncer inhaled to appear the taller of the two. He failed.

Meanwhile, Rudolph had found his glasses and was haltingly waving at Tammy. He stabbed the air in the scav's direction with frantic thrusts of his bony forefinger. Tammy blinked audibly at the dealer, and then sprinted (as much as one can in 3-inch heels) to block the door before the scav could leave. "Waitaminute bub, you don't talk like a desert scav."

"And you don't look like a prostitute. Move it." He pushed her aside with ease and was five long strides outside before Christina joined her Ranger friend at the door.

"You two know him? Is he---"

"He said the 'f'-word," Tammy interrupted casually, squinting through her cheap mascara and the glare. With both pistols in one hand, she began to strip off her boots.

"Huh?" Christina had never heard Tammy utter a non sequitur. "Oh, that one. So?? You got a copyright?"

Tammy shoved her boots and wig into Christina's taut gut before chasing after the would-be scav barefoot. Her words floated in the air in her wake: "It's been standard Ranger-speak ever since Jax and and 'Lestro enlisted."

"Who's Jax?" she said to Butkus, who raised his single brow. Christina gave him Tammy's paraphernalia. "Be a good bouncer and stick these behind the bar." And she trotted off after the erstwhile hooker.

Chapter 5
Bernard Assaf

CLUB ACAPULCO, NEEDLES: Rudolph dusted himself off and walked over to Spanky. "Sorry about that, Spanky," he said sincerely, jerking a thumb at what was left of the card table behind him.

Spanky laughed as he stashed his M17 carbine under the back lip of the bar. "No problem, I've been meaning to replace that poker table anyway. Speaking of which, I guess I'll pay for the table out of your paycheck this month." He paused to smile sincerely. "Thanks!" Spanky grinned mischievously and Rudolph groaned disappointedly.

Rudolph had been deliberately cheating the desert scavenger that he'd identified as Ishmael, who was absent without leave, according to Ranger reports. Of course, he tried to cheat everyone who came to his table, at least one or two times. He hadn't expected Ishmael to catch him at it, and certainly didn't think he'd get roughed up as he did. Maybe with the undercover Rangers in the room, he felt cocky and got too careless? He wasn't sure.

The dealer sat down at a barstool and considered the events that had led up to today. Rudolph had been contacted by the Rangers a few months back, and had been handed a photo and a name. If he were to spot the man in the photo, he was to call them, and he'd be paid handsomely for his help.

The man in the picture had become a regular over the past few weeks, but it wasn't until he'd overheard someone call him "Ish" that he took a second look, and finally recognized him. Ishmael had given that someone a harsh look and hadn't returned for a week, but eventually started showing up again, and with a different hair color and demeanor. Why he didn't just leave town if he was on the run, Rudolph couldn't say, but maybe the guy was addicted to gambling, or didn't want to leave town just yet. Speculation aside, Rudolph had remembered the face and had called the Rangers, and arranged for them to show up today so he could point Ishmael out to them.

The plan was for the Rangers to arrive sometime in the three o'clock hour. They'd approach Rudolph when they were ready to make their move. They had never approached him; maybe they hadn't counted Ishmael catching Rudolph cheating at the deal either. Rudolph had asked them why they didn't just barge in and grab him, now that they had a positive sighting for him.

The Ranger contacts explained they preferred a more clandestine operation, and Rudolph understood why. The New Nevada Desert Rangers were getting a bad reputation with some of their number operating a bit too independently and murderously for the public comfort. Rudolph knew of a few bad apples, for sure, but overall thought of the Rangers as a force for good in the Wasteland, and respected what they had to put up with and do to keep the peace.

Rudolph looked at his watch. Three-fifteen. He sighed. He regretted that he never had a chance to hook up with the Ranger contacts, but he guessed the two women who saved his life, and who had subsequently run after Ishmael, were the contacts. Dressed as a prostitute and a mercenary. He chuckled to himself, and thought, "Great disguises, they fit right in with the rest of the scum of this town." Sighing, he stood up and told Spanky he'd like to serve a double-shift. He would need the extra cash, especially until his bounty came in. If it came in. For now, he'd trust that the Rangers would make good on their end, and hoped they caught up to this "Ish" fellow. Whatever he was on the run from, he hoped the Rangers would bring him to justice.

* * *

OUTSIDE CLUB ACAPULCO, NEEDLES. Tammy chased after Ishmael, who had run toward the west side of Downtown Needles, and whom Rudolph had pointed out as their missing Ranger back at the Acapulco Club. "Frakkit," she cursed, "he must be on to us!" She ran after him, and reached behind her back to pull out a small radio transmitter from under her mini-skirt's hem. Bringing it to her mouth, she activated it and spoke quickly as Ishmael made a sudden cut south into the alleyway between Leory's and the Club.

"Dark Angel to Moon Base, positive I. D. on Pigeon. He's in flight toward the east end of the train! Wearing sunglasses and a green mechanic's jumpsuit! I'm in pursuit, move in!"

It was important that the Rangers bring Ishmael in, for reasons Tammy had no plans to divulge to Christina at this time. She was sent in to identify Ishmael, and then radio for backup on the assumption that he would offer resistance. She had asked her friend from before she signed up with the Rangers, Christina, for a little in-house backup in case things got ugly. The situation at Acapulco had become as ugly as her make-up job, so she was glad Christina had agreed to meet her. She wondered how the Rangers would react if they found out Tammy had pulled in some mercenary help.

As she turned to go south, she tripped on some scrub at what was left of the mostly disintegrated curb. After all, she was barefoot. Suddenly a Ranger in full camoflauge and equipment appeared. He paused briefly to help Tammy stand, smirking at her outfit, then bolted off between the buildings after Ishmael. Tammy gladly let the other Rangers pick up the pursuit. Christina caught up to her a few moments later.

"Outta breath, Private?" Christina joked. The alleyway where they paused was overgrown with thick grass, but had a worn trail right down the middle. It was obviously traveled often, and from the number of bullet holes in the sides of the buildings, was a place where mercenary activity was high. As Tammy glanced at the bullet holes, Christina looked up and chewed her lip innocently.
